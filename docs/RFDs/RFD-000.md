# 1. Executive Summary

The Claire Performance Engine (CPE) introduces a dedicated communication intelligence layer into the Claire Project.

Rather than treating speech synthesis as a direct conversion from text to audio, CPE establishes an intermediate stage that determines **how speech should be performed** before any acoustic model generates sound.

This separation enables backend-independent expressive reasoning, reusable communication intelligence, and a stable foundation for future acoustic model research.

CPE does not generate audio.

CPE determines the ideal performance of spoken language.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 2. Background

Traditional Text-to-Speech (TTS) systems tightly couple communication reasoning with acoustic generation.

As a result:

- expressive behavior becomes model-specific,
- communication improvements require retraining acoustic models,
- expressive reasoning cannot easily be reused across backends,
- comparison between acoustic models becomes difficult.

The Claire Project separates communication reasoning from acoustic synthesis through an independent Performance Engine.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 3. Problem Statement

Current speech synthesis systems generally suffer from the following limitations:

- Communication understanding and speech generation are tightly coupled.
- Expressive behavior depends on individual acoustic models.
- Backend switching often changes expressive output.
- Improvements in communication reasoning require modifications to synthesis models.
- Existing architectures provide limited opportunities for backend-independent research.

These limitations reduce extensibility, portability, and long-term maintainability.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 4. Research Objectives

The objectives of the Claire Performance Engine are:

- Separate communication reasoning from speech synthesis.
- Define a backend-independent representation of speech performance.
- Enable expressive speech across multiple compatible backends.
- Establish a stable interface between communication intelligence and acoustic rendering.
- Provide the research foundation for the future Claire Acoustic Model (CAM).

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 5. Scope

- Native support for the future Claire Acoustic Model.

## Out of Scope

- Vocoders.
- Acoustic inference.
- Phoneme generation.
- Voice cloning.
- Backend implementation.
- Unity implementation details.

-------------------------------------------------------------------------------

# 6. Guiding Principles

The Claire Performance Engine SHALL operate according to the principles established in the Claire Project Constitution.

In particular:

- Communication reasoning SHALL remain independent of acoustic synthesis.
- Performance SHALL remain backend-independent.
- Compatible backends SHALL approximate performance according to their capabilities.
- Additional context SHALL improve performance but SHALL never be required.
- Simplicity SHALL be preferred over unnecessary complexity.

# 8. Proposed Research Direction

The Claire Performance Engine receives a **Performance Context** consisting of:

Required:

- Text

Optional:

- Character State

When Character State is provided, CPE SHALL use it as contextual information.

When Character State is absent, CPE SHALL infer the required performance from the supplied text.

CPE produces a backend-independent **Performance Graph**.

Compatible backends translate the Performance Graph according to their supported capabilities.

The Claire Acoustic Model is designed to consume the complete Performance Graph natively.
